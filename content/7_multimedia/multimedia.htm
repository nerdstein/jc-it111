Multimedia / Hypermedia

Defined

You may have heard of the terms multimedia and hypermedia.  Do you know what they mean?  If we go to dictionary.com, we see the following:

 

Media (with many definitions):

  1. Something, such as an intermediate course of action, that occupies a position or represents a condition midway between extremes.
  2. An intervening substance through which something else is transmitted or carried on.
  3. An agency by which something is accomplished, conveyed, or transferred: The train was the usual medium of transportation in those days.
  4. pl. media Usage Problem.
    1. A means of mass communication, such as newpapers, magazines, radio, or television.
    2. media (used with a sing. or pl. verb) The group of journalists and others who constitute the communications industry and profession.
  5. pl. media Computer Science. An object or device, such as a disk, on which data is stored.
  6. pl. mediums A person thought to have the power to communicate with the spirits of the dead or with agents of another world or dimension. Also called psychic.
  7. pl. media
    1. A surrounding environment in which something functions and thrives.
    2. The substance in which a specific organism lives and thrives.
    3. A culture medium.
  8.  
    1. A specific kind of artistic technique or means of expression as determined by the materials used or the creative methods involved: the medium of lithography.
    2. The materials used in a specific artistic technique: oils as a medium.
  9. A solvent with which paint is thinned to the proper consistency.
  10. Chemistry. A filtering substance, such as filter paper.
  11. A size of paper, usually 18 × 23 inches or 17 1/2 × 22 inches.

 

Multi:

pref.

  1. Many; much; multiple: multicolored.
  2.  
    1. More than one: multiparous.
    2. More than two: multilateral.

Hyper :

 

The term hyper is a little more elusive in its applicable definition.  However, if we look at the various synonyms, we see:

“above, after, ahead, apart from, away from, before, behind, besides, clear of, farther, free of, hyper, more remote, moreover, outside, over, over there, past, remote, superior to, without, yonder”

From the Greek, the term hyper means – over, above, beyond

So you see that multimedia could be thought of as “many forms of information transmission” and hypermedia is literally translated as “the means of going beyond the traditional structures of information transmission”.

In truth, in the information age, we tend to be very comfortable with multimedia and hypermedia.  The idea of going beyond or further, implies incorporating many different forms of video, audio, photography, text, animation, and design into an interlinked interface.  How many forms of information transmission can you think of?

  • Writing (Text)
  • Voice (telephone, recordings)
  • Video (TV, Film)
  • Others?

Issues with Multimedia/Hypermedia Applications

 

There are MANY advantages and otherwise “cool” things about multimedia applications.  The most obvious being the visualizations.  Many people tend to learn better from visualizations – plus they look really cool!  However, as with any good thing, there are often bad things.  In the world of multimedia, the main issues tend to be cost – in the sense that the various media have a tendency to be larger in term sof storage and transmission requirements.  The very nature of multimedia files is that they tend to be fairly large – at least relatively speaking.  Take any MS WORD file and insert a few images and resave it.  The size goes up – this is obvious.  Images require more storage space than text and video requires more than single images.  So as far as storage is concerned, we tend to need more for multimedia and this costs money.  Fortunately, the cost of storage is dropping and we can store more and more data at a far lesser cost. Transmission of the data and files is also a cost issue.  Since the files are larger, they would also tend to take longer to send and require more bandwidth.  Think about our own network here at Juniata College.  What are the bottlenecks in the transmission of large multimedia files:

 

  1. From you to your roommate?
  2. From you to a friend across campus?
  3. From you to a friend at another college?

 

Regardless of the issues with multimedia, it is still true that the advantages far outweigh the disadvantages.  As part of this topic you will be introduced to one or more multimedia applications – getting hands on experience.
